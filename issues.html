Issues

To guage your political opinions, we selected flagship political issues and incoporated questions about them into our survey. Below is an explanation of each of the questions, and what different responses mean.

Political Affiliation

Political Affiliation refers to your party association-- whether you identify as a Democrat, Republican, or Independent. We won't use your response here when we calculate how well any given legistlator represents you. Though members of the same political party tend to agree with each other, we cannot assume that a legistlator of your party represents you in any way just because they are a member of your party. If you don't identify with any of these parties, you can skip the question. To understand what each party represents, you can visit the official websites of the U.S. political parties: Republicans, Democrats, Independents.

Gun Control

Gun Control refers to your stance on the 2nd Amendment in the United States, which gives the American people the right to bear arms - the right to own firearms. To further understand the breakdown of Gun Control, you can read a helpful Pew Research Article here. The responses to the Gun Control question in our survey account for the four most common arguments, but we know that there are other responses too. Read about how we address this problem on our Limitations section.

Abortion

Abortion is "the procuring of premature delivery so as to destroy offspring." People tend to hold one of two views on abortion: pro-life or pro-choice. The pro-life argument is anti-abortion, generally under the premise that abortion is morally wrong given the intrinsic value of the embryo's life as a living being. The pro-choice argument is believes that a woman should have the right to have an abortion, generally under the premise that abortion is ethical given the underdeveloped, dependent nature of the embryo during pregnancy. These two views account for both of the responses in our question regarding abortion.

Wages

Minimum wage is the minimum rate at which one is required to pay a worker for their labor or services. The selectable answers to the wages question in our survey account for almost all possible stances on wage: the hourly minimum wage should be over $10; in between $7.25 and $10; $7.25 or lower; and finally, there should be no minimum wage. Our question here refers to federal minimum wage, not state minimum wage.
